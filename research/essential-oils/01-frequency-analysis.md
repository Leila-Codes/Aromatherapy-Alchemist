# Essential Oil Selection Process
While there are more than 150 essential oils available on the market, we must confine the number of essential oils identified and researched for the project in order to keep a suitable scope and ensure the project can be delivered on time. The below sections will outline how essential oils have been selected for inclusion in the app.

## Method
Following search terms on the internet "most popular essential oils" and variations of, the following grey literature articles were identified:

- https://aromatherapyanywhere.com/list-of-essential-oils-and-uses/
- https://naha.org/explore-aromatherapy/about-aromatherapy/most-commonly-used-essential-oils
- https://health.clevelandclinic.org/essential-oils-101-do-they-work-how-do-you-use-them
- https://moodessentialoils.co.uk/blogs/essential-oils/what-are-the-12-must-have-essential-oils
- https://www.edensgarden.com/blogs/news/what-are-the-10-most-popular-essential-oils
- https://miskiyafragrance.com/top-10-most-popular-essential-oils
- https://www.hollandandbarrett.com/the-health-hub/natural-beauty/aromatherapy/the-7-most-popular-essential-oils/
- https://emidesignstudio.com/blog/blogs/25-most-popular-essential-oils

Totalling **8** Articles.

These articles were consolidated into a list of essential oils reported by each article, before undertaking frequency analysis to count the number of times each essential oil was reported across these sources.
